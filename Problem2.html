My Experience with Baking Macarons

Undertaking the mere act of attempting to bake macarons is a virtue. It is not for the faint of heart. It is definitely not where an amateur baker should attempt to foray. They are not even one of the most delicious desserts. So what would possess anyone in their right mind to make them?

For me, it's about the feeling of accomplishment rather than the end product, which is antithetical to the tenets of baking. To say that I mastered macarons is a badge of honor I wear proudly.

Ingredients for Macarons

Basic Ingredients

The ingredient list is short and simple which is a kind gesture considering how complex they are to bake. Macarons require almond flour, egg whites, powdered sugar, and granulated sugar. Optional ingredients would be food coloring and any flavoring agents like cocoa powder.

Filling Ingredients

This is where the bulk of flavor comes from. The cookies are more difficult to imbue with flavor than the filling. The filling is very versatile and can be made with a variety of ingredients. You can use buttercreams, ganache, jams, or curds.

Macaron Baking Techniques

Making the Shell

Making the cookie batter can be divided into predominantly two steps: making the meringue and the macaronage process. A strong meringue is the foundation to your cookie and sets the precedent to your cookie's height, presence of feet, and fullness. Paradoxically, once you build the meringue, you will enter the macaronage process where you deflate all of that air you just whipped in.

Did You Know?

The macaron is completely separate from the coconut macaroon but easily confused if one pronounces them identically.
